Managing residual symptoms major depressive disorder

Introduction

Major depressive disorder (MDD) is one of the worldâ€™s leading causes of disability. It affects individuals, families, and society and is common in patients seeking care in the primary care setting. Despite these challenges, MDD can be effectively managed. However the current definition of remission does not fully take into account all aspects of patient recovery. Residual symptoms of depression are very common in patients who are classified as being in remission. Patients with residual symptoms are at increased risk of functional and interpersonal impairments, and are at high risk for recurrence of depression. In this virtual patient activity provided by med-sims.org by SEI Heathcare, four renowned thought leaders address the optimal approach to identifying the most common, as well as less common, residual symptoms of MDD.

Target audience

This activity is intended for the attendees of the EPA Psychiatry Congress 2018, psychiatrists, psychologists, primary care physicians and other healthcare professionals practicing outside the United States.

Overview

In this interactive case simulation series you will encounter realistic clinical scenarios focused on diagnosis and the management of the residual symptoms of MDD.

Goal

To update psychiatrists, psychologists, primary care physicians and other healthcare professionals on recent advances in the management of residual symptoms in patients with MDD.
